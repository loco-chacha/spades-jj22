♠️ Spades Strategy Guide

1. Before the First Card

  • 🧮Add up all bids on the table.
  • If total bid ≥ 12 → play aggressively to take every trick and set opponents.
  • If total bid ≤ 10 → play selectively, save low cards to escape leads, and avoid wasting lowest cards under opponents’ tricks.
  • If total bid = 11 → take early tricks, adjust later for bags or to push for extra tricks.

2. Don’t Get Set 🚫

Getting set is the biggest penalty in Spades—much worse than a few bags.

  • ⚖️Focus on risk vs. reward.
  • One set often costs 100+ points, while 19 bags only cost -81 net.
  • 🛡️Taking extra tricks protects your team’s bid and may set opponents.

3. Follow the Score 📊

Adjust bids based on what is necessary to win, especially in final hands.

  • If you need 4 tricks to win, but only see 3 in your hand—bid 4 anyway.
  • Small math decisions often swing entire games.

4. Team Play 🤝

  • Bids are team bids, not individual.
  • Support partner’s weak plays (cover lost Aces/Kings).
  • Don’t steal tricks your partner is already winning.
  • If you want solo play, try Cutthroat Spades.

5. Adjusting to Partner’s Bid 🎯

  • If partner bids 1 → assume 0, bid lower and cover extra.
  • If partner bids NIL → make covering NIL the top priority.
  • Sacrifice tricks (e.g., lead Kings) if necessary to cover.

6. Risk and Score Management ⚠️

  • If leading by 100+, avoid risky NILs.
  • Adjust bids: conservative when leading, aggressive when behind.
  • Do not overbid to “prove your hand.” Only bid what is needed to secure the win.

7. Big Hands & Overlap 🔍

  • High total team bids carry higher risk.
  • If partner bids 4 and you see 5, consider bidding 4 to avoid overlap.
  • A failed 9-bid (-90) vs. a safe 8-bid (+80) = 170-point swing!

8. Bag Game Myth 🎭

Strong players don’t “play for bags.” They play to set you—and bag you only when they can’t set you.

9. Never Give Up 💪

Even if far behind, fight until mathematically eliminated.

10. Advanced Rules & Tools 🧠

  • Third-Seat High Rule – cover partner’s low lead with high card on 11+ bids.
  • Rule of 12 – only bid table to 12+ if you truly have one extra trick.
  • Cut Tricks – bid cautiously on trump tricks, as opponents may void too.
  • Spade Count Guideline – Spades = count - 3, +1 for A/K/Q.
  • Last Trick Feature – use previous trick info to track cards.
  • Play with Purpose – every card = a soldier, send with intention.

11. NIL Bidding ❌

Avoid these risky NIL situations unless desperate:

  • A + 2 cards in a suit (e.g., A32).
  • 3-card suit with lowest ≥ 7 (e.g., J87).
  • 3+ spades with lowest ≥ 6 (e.g., 976).
  • Stand-alone K or Kx (very dangerous).
  • 4+ spades (only in rare/high-bid cover cases).
  • K of spades NIL → very rare, only safe with strong partner cover.
  • 2 spades ≥ 8 (e.g., J9) = highly risky.
